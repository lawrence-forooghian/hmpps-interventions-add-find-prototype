Thinking skills programme

The Thinking Skills Programme (TSP) is a cognitive skills programme for men and women designed to help develop skills in:

  • pro-social problem solving
  • perspective taking
  • developing and managing relationships
  • self-management

It also encourages pro-social attitudes, behaviour and goals for the future.

TSP has been designed to incorporate maximum responsivity and flexibility of delivery format.

New me strengths (for sex offenders)

NMS is a cognitive-behavioural group-work programme suitable for adult men who have been convicted for any offence, and are assessed as having a medium risk or above of reconviction. This programme is suitable for both custody and community delivery and is the LDC suite equivalent of the Thinking Skills Programme (TSP), Horizon, Building Better Relationships (BBR) and Resolve.

Delivery of habit modules from MSRP

Built from sessions selected from a menu of 48 sessions across 11 modules, the programme aims to support those with substance misuse issues. It is not a replacement or substitute for alcohol or drug abuse programmes but can be used in conjunction with them. It addresses some basic factors around addiction and seeks to build self-reliance and inner strength for those battling with addiction problems.
